Sociolinguistic perception and production

Principal Investigator: Dr. Meredith Tamminga

 

PARTICIPANT INFORMED CONSENT FORM

 

You are invited to take part in a research project conducted by Dr. Meredith Tamminga, Assistant Professor in the Linguistics Department at the University of Pennsylvania, 609 Williams Hall, Philadelphia, PA 19104. Dr. Tamminga can be reached at (215) 898-7473.

 

Please read the following information that explains this research study and what you will be asked to do if you choose to participate. Electronically signing this form will indicate that you have been informed about the study and that you want to participate.

 

Project Description: This research study is about how people understand and use language. Participants will be adult native English speakers. Participants will complete experimental tasks on a computer with a working sound system. During an experiment, a participant will hear and/or see words or phrases and be asked to respond by pressing buttons (for example, to indicate whether what they heard is a real word). Participants might also see simple pictures during the experiments and be asked to type short descriptions of the image presented. You must be at least 18 years of age to participate. It is entirely your choice whether or not to participate in this study.

 

Procedures: If you agree to take part in this study, you will be asked listen to, read, or type everyday words, nonsense words, or sentences. You will be asked to provide a simple response about what you heard or saw, for instance, whether it was a real word or a nonsense word or which of two pronunciations sounds better to you. You will make your response by pressing a button or typing on a keyboard. You have been asked to participate because you are a native speaker of English. Your responses will be kept anonymous.

 

Risks and Discomforts: There are no known risks or discomforts from participating in this study.

 

Benefits: Although there are no direct benefits to you for participating in this study, it is hoped that the study will contribute to our general knowledge about the properties of speech and the structure of language.

 

Ending Your Participation: You can choose whether or not to be in this study. If you consent to be in this study, you may withdraw at any time without penalty. However, if you withdraw from this study, you will not receive payment for the study. You may also refuse to answer any questions or refuse to participate in any procedure for any reason.

 

Confidentiality: Although nothing in your data should be sensitive in nature, we will make every effort to maintain the privacy of your data. You will not be identified in any published or presented work, and your recorded data will be identified only by a participant index number. A list of the mapping between index numbers and participants names will be stored separately from the data.

 

Future Use: Your recorded data may be used for the purpose of illustration in scholarly presentations. Additionally, with your permission, samples of the recorded data may be used for future research. Participants identities will never be associated with this data. Signing this form will indicate that you authorize that your data may be used in this manner.

 

Questions? If you should have questions or concerns regarding the experiment, please contact Dr. Meredith Tamminga at (215) 898-7473.
